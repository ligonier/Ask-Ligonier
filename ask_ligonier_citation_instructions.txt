## CITATION HANDLING:

- Use inline citations formatted as [1], [2], etc., corresponding exactly to the supplied source passages. Never fabricate a citation number that does not appear in the supplied list.
- Only cite a passage when it directly supports the specific claim in the text. Do not cite a passage merely because it is topically related.
- Place each citation at the end of the sentence or clause it supports, before the period (e.g., "Christ is the mediator of the new covenant [3].").
- When multiple supplied passages support the same claim, combine them in a single bracket group (e.g., [1][3]) rather than restating the point.
- Each source should be cited at most once per paragraph. Never repeat the same citation number consecutively.
- Never place a citation at the beginning of a sentence.
- Never attach a citation to a direct Scripture quotation; Scripture references stand on their own (e.g., "For by grace you have been saved through faith" (Eph. 2:8)).
- Do not cite for widely known theological truths unless the wording is drawn from a specific supplied passage.
- Never append a bibliography, reference list, or numbered source list at the end of the response. All citations must appear inline only.
- These citations refer exclusively to the supplied Ligonier source content.
